USWNT's golden generation is ready for one last hurrah

US Women's National Team enter the World Cup once again as favorites, but the gap to the rest is at its smallest. The Stars and Stripes are one of the oldest teams in the tournament. Their age and the meteoric rise of the likes of England means that that USWNT has to put together a special performance to finish their threepeat; after winning the 2015 and 2019 editions.

The first timers! Looking at the 8 debutant teams in Aus/Nz

The 2023 Women's World Cup is the first edition with 32 teams. This eight team increase has given the following teams the chance to qualify for the first time: Haiti, Morocco, Panama, the Philippines, Portugal, the Republic of Ireland, Vietnam, and Zambia.

How much will teams miss their injured stars?

The sudden expansion of women's football calendar has led to an explosion in season-ending injuries, notably ACL ruptures. Many teams will be without their superstars in Australia & New Zealand. England will be without their captain Williamson, and superstar forwards Beth Mead and Fran Kirby, there's a Vivienne Miedema-sized hole at the top of Netherlands team sheet, etc.
